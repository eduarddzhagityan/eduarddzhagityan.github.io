Professional Profile

Eduard P. Dzhagityan is a financial economist specializing in macrofinancial regulation, banking system structure, monetary policy, systemic risk, financial stability, and banking mergers and acquisitions (M&A). His professional background combines more than twenty-five years of senior experience in international banking and financial institutions with over a decade of academic research and teaching.

His academic work focuses on the institutional architecture and operational mechanisms of banking regulation and financial stability, the interaction between monetary policy and macroprudential regulation, and the systemic risk implications of modern banking sectors, among other topics. His research agenda includes the development of a macrofinancial and quantitative risk-focused regulatory framework for banking M&A, integrating systemic risk diagnostics, M&A stress testing, and quantitative measurement of post-M&A integration resilience.
